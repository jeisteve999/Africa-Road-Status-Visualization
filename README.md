# Africa Road Infrastructure Analysis
Open Government Africa — FRST Dataset Dashboard
Overview

This project presents an exploratory data analysis of road infrastructure across multiple African countries using the FRST dataset, a large-scale road network dataset derived from OpenStreetMap and machine learning models.

The objective of this analysis is to understand road distribution, accessibility, and infrastructure disparities across African regions and countries.

This dashboard highlights:

Total road length by country
Paved vs. unpaved road distribution
Top and bottom countries by road infrastructure
Regional comparisons across Africa
Share of total road infrastructure
Road accessibility and infrastructure gaps


# Data Source

For this analysis, I used the FRST dataset, which is based on OpenStreetMap and machine learning models to estimate road networks across countries.

This type of data is highly valuable for spatial and exploratory analysis, as it captures:

Formal roads
Informal roads
Residential streets
Rural access roads
Secondary transportation networks

This provides a broader view of accessibility and infrastructure beyond official datasets.

However, it is important to note that this dataset may not fully align with official statistics due to differences in mapping coverage and methodology.

-For example:

In my analysis, Egypt appears to have a larger road network than Nigeria. However, this likely reflects higher mapping density and urban coverage rather than actual infrastructure superiority.

Countries with higher urban density or better mapping activity may appear to have more total road length, even if official infrastructure investment differs.

-Although the dataset is not intended for official infrastructure reporting, it is highly valuable for:

Identifying accessibility gaps
Supporting infrastructure planning
Evaluating regional connectivity
Enabling data-driven decision making

Even if total road length differs from official statistics, the dataset helps identify how road density and distribution affect:

Accessibility
Economic activity
Urban development
Regional growth

# Key Insights

This analysis highlights significant disparities in road infrastructure across African countries and regions.

The findings suggest that increased investment in road infrastructure could significantly improve:

Trade competitiveness
Transportation efficiency
Regional connectivity
Economic growth
Employment opportunities
Import and export capacity
Access to markets and services

-Improving road infrastructure across these regions would strengthen Africa’s position in global trade, enhance regional integration, and support long-term economic development across the continent.
